Inside Streetcar

You got on the streetcar, now what?

- As always, tap your form of transit at any door. If the door is closing on you, click the red button. If you need a physical ticket/transfer, there’s a machine in the middle

- When choosing a seat, move down until you find a seat. Leave the blue seats; for those who need them, there’s plenty of space to stand and plenty of seats.

- Don’t stand in front of the Presto machine; people need to use it, and don’t stand in front of the driver’s door, as they will need space to exit

- Your bag doesn’t need a seat

- Feet off the seats

- Make space for those getting off by getting up or moving your feet.

- When it's crowded, and you’re closest to the door, get off, so the people behind you can get off and then get back on instead of standing there like a fool blocking the door.

- When wanting to get off, you can press the stop buttons on the poles or press the circular open door button that lights up green on the door

- Before stepping off, look out for crazy drivers
Streetcars can be a chill ride until Fair Enforcers come on and the whole vibe changes. Just make sure you have your proof of payment, like your Presto or ticket. Understandably, they're doing their job, but some are so entitled and confrontational. Warning: if you're using a youth, student, or senior (who Fair Enforcers usually don’t pick on), Presto pass, prepare to show ID, prove your age, and laugh in their faces when they can’t give you a 100-dollar-plus fare-evasion ticket.
